# Engage Partners — Full Playbook Reference

## /establish-hypothesis

### What is a hypothesis?
A hypothesis captures an early belief about what might be happening or what could be possible. It creates a starting point for product discovery. The PM may write one on their own, co-create it, or receive it from a partner.

### Quality criteria — singular, clear, testable
- **Singular**: Focuses on only one idea worth exploring
- **Clear**: Easy to understand without explanation
- **Testable**: Framed to be confirmed, challenged, or refined

### Writing pattern
"There may be an opportunity to improve how [people or systems] do [an action] during [when] at [where]."
Keep it to 1–2 sentences in plain everyday language. Let the "why" and "how" emerge through learning.

### Red flags
- Describes a solution → reframe as an observation about what people experience
- Covers multiple ideas → split into separate singular hypotheses
- Too vague to test → push for specificity about who, what, where, when

### Output format
A single-sentence or two-sentence hypothesis statement, followed by a quality check against all three criteria.

## /create-discovery-plan

### What is an insight plan?
An insight plan clarifies what you need to understand, who or what can best help you learn it, and confirms that any additional insights not in the plan would add volume, not clarity.

### Three perspectives to cover

| Perspective | Focus | Key questions |
|---|---|---|
| **Experience** | How the current experience is perceived and felt by Guests, Cast, Crew, and business partners | Who is most impacted? What feels difficult, frustrating, or inconsistent? |
| **Technology** | How systems, platforms, or tools behave and how that affects experience and outcomes | How do the systems involved behave in practice? Where do constraints, failures, or dependencies appear? |
| **Business** | How the situation impacts performance, operations, costs, revenue, or efficiency | What outcomes are affected? Who feels the impact if nothing changes? |

### Completeness checklist
- [ ] You know what you need to learn about your hypothesis
- [ ] You've identified who or what can best help you learn it
- [ ] Any additional insights not in the plan would add volume, not clarity

### Special DXT services
- **Proof of Value (POV)**: For hypotheses needing insights on emerging technology not yet available at Disney Experiences
- **DX Technology Experience Council (DX-TEC)**: When the hypothesis primarily involves operational Cast or Crew Members, or when known pain points are described second-hand rather than observed

### Output format
A structured insight plan table with: Learning need | Perspective | Source/Partner | Method (interview, data request, observation, etc.)

## /create-stakeholder-map

### Who to engage
Review the hypothesis and identify partners across experience, technology, and business who hold the insight needed to confirm or challenge it.

### Directly impacted partners
- Guests, Cast, or Crew Members who experience the situation today
- Systems or tools that are part of the affected workflow
- Team members closest to the problem in daily operations

### Indirectly impacted partners
- Leaders or teams who feel downstream consequences
- Partners who have to explain, fix, or recover when things go wrong
- Stakeholders who influence whether a solution is viable or approved

### Engagement approach by partner type
| Partner type | Best engagement approach |
|---|---|
| Front-line Cast / Crew | DX-TEC session, observation, structured interview |
| Technology owners | Technical discovery session, system walkthrough, Microsoft Teams call |
| Business leaders | Stakeholder interview, data request, Microsoft Teams meeting |
| Experience / Design | Co-creation session, journey walk, Teams whiteboard session |

**Coordination tip:** Schedule discovery sessions via Microsoft Teams meetings. Use Teams channels to share insight summaries asynchronously and keep partners aligned between sessions. Follow up with Outlook email for formal requests or when partners are outside your immediate Teams workspace.

### Output format
A stakeholder map table: Partner Name/Role | Perspective (Experience/Technology/Business) | Direct or Indirect | What they can help you learn | Recommended engagement approach

## /summarize-discovery

### What to look for in gathered insights

**Patterns** — signals or behaviors that show up repeatedly across people, situations, or data sources. They help identify what may be consistently true and increase confidence that something is worth deeper consideration.

**Variations** — meaningful differences in how people, systems, or situations behave under different conditions. They help surface constraints, edge cases, and explain why a pattern may not affect everyone the same way.
